Self Confidence

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

According to Daniel Goleman, self-awareness is knowing one's internal states, preferences, resources and intuitions. And he also said 'Self-awareness... is a neutral mode that maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions.', which is very similar to what Jon Kabat-Zinn defined mindfulness:'Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non- judgementally.' As they are pretty much same thing, training for mindfulness will enhance self-awareness too, which can help improving self-confidence.

Try at least one of the self awareness practices from the chapter (Body Scan, Journaling). What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

I actually try body scan sometimes before I go to sleep, especially when I have too many thoughts or somehow I can't fall asleep. I learned it from yoga teacher but I think it's very similar to what this book explains. It relaxes my body and get me easy to fall asleep as it explains in the book. I haven't tried journaling

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

At first time, it was hard to understand, but I feel that strong emotions like anger or sadness control myself sometimes. I should always try to remember that the feelings are just what I experience, not myself. The metaphor that thoughts and emotions are like clouds made it easy to understand.
